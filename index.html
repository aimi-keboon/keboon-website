The problem

Local food supply and local demand are disconnected.

Consumers want fresher, more affordable and transparent produce. Growers need better visibility, direct buyers and stronger income opportunities. Existing channels often leave both sides separated by logistics, intermediaries or fragmented informal networks.

Consumers lack visibility

People may want to support local growers, but often do not know who is growing food nearby, what is available, or how to connect directly.

Growers lack direct access

Urban growers, small farmers and home gardeners may have produce to sell but limited digital visibility, inconsistent demand and small margins.

Supply chains are inefficient

Fresh produce often depends on longer distribution routes, third-party channels and logistics that can increase costs and reduce transparency.

The solution

A hyperlocal food platform built around proximity.

Keboon starts with a directory and grows into a marketplace: growers list what they have, consumers discover what is nearby, and communities build stronger local food networks.

Ecosystem vision

Not just a marketplace. A local food ecosystem.

Keboon starts by connecting local growers and consumers directly, then expands into a broader support system that strengthens community participation, partnerships, and future agricultural innovation.

Market opportunity

Malaysia-first, with Southeast Asia expansion potential.

Keboon sits at the intersection of fresh produce commerce, digital marketplaces, local food distribution, urban farming, sustainability and food security.

Why now?

Several market forces are converging: food price pressure, interest in local sourcing, sustainability awareness, digital marketplace behaviour and growing food security concerns.

Market wedge

Keboon does not need to replace supermarkets or wet markets. It creates a new digital layer for local food discovery and community-based access.


Initial market: selected Malaysian urban and suburban communities.


Expansion path: Malaysia-wide clusters → B2B local sourcing → Southeast Asia pilots → global local-food ecosystem model.
